Commentiquette - Reach for the Top!

Because a YouTube comments section of a music video is the highest echelon of online discourse.

Made by a guy who resides on sobercoder.me. Contact me there and suggest better videos as datasets (preferably more than 10000 comments, and in English).


Inspiration

Humbly inspired by Erik Hoffstad's brilliant internet series Internet Comment Etiquette, where Erik tries his damnest to promote proper internet comment etiquette. Commentiquette tries his best.

It's a simple RNN model built in Tensorflow and Keras, containing three layers: an Embedding layer, a GRU layer, and a hidden (dense) layer. It's trained on datasets I make myself using the Youtube V3 API for comments.
The whole thing is put in some funky Flask framework, and tied together with some glue and sticks at the end.
Obviously, it's in super-alpha or whatever 'super experimental' means; the loosely-held together Bootstrap 4 says it all. Right now, it only provides pre-trained models that I spend my precious CPU training for over a hundred epochs. My NVIDIA GeForce GTX 1650 isn't CUDA Enabled (or is, depending on which forum you read). In the future, I wish to provide functionality through the front-end that allows people to send in YouTube links for it to be trained later, if that's even realistically possible. I even installed cudNN, which required signing up for NVIDIA Developers and failing three Captchas.
